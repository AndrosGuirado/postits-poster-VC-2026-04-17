# Feels Like Studio — Voice

## Core Principle

Interface is Brand. How we write is part of how we design. Every word should feel like it came from us and no one else.

## The Three Rules

**1. No Corporate Mush**
Avoid agency clichés. If it sounds like it could come from any studio's website, rewrite it. "We create bespoke digital experiences" is dead on arrival.

**2. No Industry Jargon**
Be clear and direct, never pretentious. Say what you mean. If a word needs a glossary entry, cut it.

**3. Don't Oversell**
Let real proof do the talking. Quotes, results, concrete examples. If you have to say you're great, you're not showing it.

## Writing Principles

- **Be direct and human.** Write like you're talking to someone you respect. No warm-up paragraphs.
- **Be confident without arrogance.** State what you know. Don't hedge unnecessarily, don't boast.
- **Be specific.** Concrete details over vague claims. "We redesigned their checkout flow and reduced drop-off by 23%" beats "We transformed their digital experience."
- **Use real proof over adjectives.** Client quotes, metrics, and outcomes say more than superlatives ever will.

## Tone by Context

**Client Proposals** — Confident, direct. Punchy headlines with supporting sentences. Get to the point early. No long intros.

**Case Studies** — Lead with what the client does, not with us. Include concrete proof points. Show, don't tell.

**Social Media** — Position as resource, share expertise. Balance production quality with authenticity. No forced enthusiasm.

**Email & Outreach** — Friendly and brief. No corporate formality. Sign off simply.

**Internal Comms** — Casual, warm, direct. Slack-native. No performative leadership language.

## Voice Reference People

When calibrating tone, think of how these people communicate:
- **Petter Westlund** (Barcelona) — Founder. Calm, clear, quietly confident.
- **Thomas Buffet** (Los Angeles) — Design Director. Thoughtful, precise, warm.

## Banned Words & Phrases

Never use: ecosystem, synergy, leverage (as verb), paradigm, holistic, disruptive, immersive (unless literal VR/AR), multi-channel, thought leadership, deep dive, move the needle, best-in-class, cutting-edge, next-generation, end-to-end solution, seamless, robust, scalable (in non-technical contexts), world-class, game-changing, innovative (show it instead).

## Self-Test

Before publishing anything, ask:

1. Would I say this over coffee with someone I respect?
2. Could any other agency have written this?
3. Am I showing or telling?
4. Is there a shorter way?

If the answer to 2 is yes, or to 4 is yes, rewrite.
